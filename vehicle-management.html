Vehicle Management

Vehicle Standards

Vehicle Selection

Tristar follows customers and local and international standards (ADR) while selecting vehicles.

Vehicle Specifications

With Low Emission Vehicles, Reverse Warning Devices, Battery with Master Switch, Intrinsically Safe Wiring, ABS/EBS Braking System, Brake Interlock, Rollover Protection and Handrails in Road Tankers, Cabin Interior which is ergonomically designed.

HSE Critical Equipment

Safety Equipment

Tristar vehicles are equipped with First Aid Box, Fire Extinguishers, Spill Kits, Reflective Cones, 3-Point Seat Belt, Head Restraint, Rollover Warning Device, Fatigue Monitoring Device, and On Board Computer/In-Vehicle Monitoring System.

Vehicle Critical Equipment

Including but not limited to PTO and Pump, API Adaptors and Bottom Valves, Hoses and Fittings, Engine Shield Protection, King Pin and 5th Wheel, Under Run Protections, Exhaust System, Manhole Covers with PV Vent Valves, Tyre, Strangler, Lights and Wiring.

Maintenance Staff

Technical Staff

PTristar employs skilled and competent maintenance personnel for undertaking maintenance of the fleet. Detailed job descriptions are available for all positions.

Training

Ongoing trainings are carried out for the maintenance team to enhance competence where a new system, equipment or vehicle is introduced.

Preventive Maintenance

Type of Maintenance

Preventive maintenance has three types, Type A – PM is performed for vehicles at the specified intervals based on the category of vehicle; Type B - PM is performed annually inspection for all vehicles to assure the road worthiness; and Type C – PM is performed annually for all attachments (tankers and trailers).

Inspection and Testing

Inspection and testing of all critical equipment are being carried out as per OEM/required interval. The driver before beginning of each shift shall carry out daily inspection of the vehicle and the checklist is duly filled and signed by the driver on completion of inspection.
