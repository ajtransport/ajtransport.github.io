Journey Management

Authorized Route

Fatigue Policy

AJ Transport developed policies on drivers’ working, driving and rest hours to ensure they do not feel fatigued while on the road.

Fatigue Monitoring

Medical fitness checks include eyesight tests from the local authority and Tristar-approved hospitals. All recruited drivers are subject to medical fitness every two years, and drivers above 45 are subject to medical fitness every year.

Route Hazard Mapping (RHM)

On-board Induction

The awareness session covers HSEQ policies, Procedure, Emergency Response, Stop Work Authority, First Aid, Firefighting, Journey Plan Guidelines, Working, Driving, Rest Hours, Bulk/Pack Product Loading/Unloading, and Product Securing.

Mentoring

Fatigue monitoring devices are installed in the vehicle, which allows the detection of early signs of fatigue and alerts the driver and the base monitoring station for any fatigue related issue.

Final Evaluation

Training Evaluation

Pre and post training evaluations are conducted for their effectiveness.

HSE Passport

Evaluating the records of a driver’s training, Incident, Disciplinary Notices, PPE issuance and Appreciation/Achievement Records. Also availability of information about Emergency Numbers, Emergency Response Procedures, and Loading/Offloading Steps.

Re-Training and Retention

Refresher Training

We conduct periodical refresher training through scheduled training calendar, Toolbox Talks, Quarterly Safety Meetings and Reflective Learning Sessions.

Drivers League System

Every month drivers are awarded and recognized through the Drivers’ League System. As per the system, drivers can earn points on merits like accident/compliant free month, number of near miss reported, and number of toolbox sessions attended.
