Driver Management
01
Recruitment
Pre-screening: The pre-screening criteria intends to evaluate soft skills, technical knowledge traffic regulations, safety awareness, practical road test,and basic English reading and writing skills.
Medical Fitness: Medical fitness checks, that includes eyesight from the local authority and Tristar approved hospitals. All recruited drivers are subject to medical fitness every two years and drivers above 45 are subject to medical fitness every year.
02
Training & Mentoring
On-board Induction: The awareness session covering HSEQ policies, Procedure, Emergency Response, Stop Work Authority First Aid, Firefighting, Journey Plan Guidelines, Working, Driving, Rest Hours, Bulk/Pack Product Loading/Unloading, and Product Securing.
Mentoring: In-cab coaching for driving skills and abilities. Under study with senior drivers for familiarization with customer locations, loading and unloading, and documentations.
03
Final Evaluation
Training Evaluation: Pre and post training evaluations are conducted for their effectiveness.
HSE Passport: Evaluating the records of a driver’s training, Incident, Disciplinary Notices, PPE issuance and Appreciation/Achievement Records. Also availability of information about Emergency Numbers, Emergency Response Procedures, and Loading/Offloading Steps.
04
Re-Training & Retention
Refresher Training: We conduct periodical refresher training through scheduled training calendar, Toolbox Talks, Quarterly Safety Meetings and Reflective Learning Sessions.
Drivers League System: Every month drivers are awarded and recognized through the Drivers’ League System. As per the system, drivers can earn points on merits like accident/compliant free month, number of near miss reported, and number of toolbox sessions attended. They can also lose points on accidents, customer complaints, traffic violations and any other disciplinary notices.
Vehicle Management
01
Vehicle Standards
Vehicle Selection: Tristar follows customers and local and international standards (ADR) while selecting vehicles.
Vehicle Specifications: With Low Emission Vehicles, Reverse Warning Devices, Battery with Master Switch, Intrinsically Safe Wiring, ABS/EBS Braking System, Brake Interlock, Rollover Protection and Handrails in Road Tankers, Cabin Interior which is ergonomically designed.
02
HSE Critical Equipment
Safety Equipment: Tristar vehicles are equipped with First Aid Box, Fire Extinguishers, Spill Kits, Reflective Cones, 3-Point Seat Belt, Head Restraint, Rollover Warning Device, Fatigue Monitoring Device, and On Board Computer/In-Vehicle Monitoring System.
Vehicle Critical Equipment: Including but not limited to PTO and Pump, API Adaptors and Bottom Valves, Hoses and Fittings, Engine Shield Protection, King Pin and 5th Wheel, Under Run Protections, Exhaust System, Manhole Covers with PV Vent Valves, Tyre, Strangler, Lights and Wiring.
03
Maintenance Staff
Technical Staff: Tristar employs skilled and competent maintenance personnel for undertaking maintenance of the fleet. Detailed job descriptions are available for all positions.
Training: Ongoing trainings are carried out for the maintenance team to enhance competence where a new system, equipment or vehicle is introduced.
04
Preventive Maintenance
Supervisors provide pre-trip Type of Maintenance: Preventive maintenance has three types, Type A – PM is performed for vehicles at the specified intervals based on the category of vehicle; Type B - PM is performed annually inspection for all vehicles to assure the road worthiness; and Type C – PM is performed annually for all attachments (tankers and trailers).
Inspection and Testing: Inspection and testing of all critical equipment are being carried out as per OEM/required interval. The driver before beginning of each shift shall carry out daily inspection of the vehicle and the checklist is duly filled and signed by the driver on completion of inspection.
Journey Management
01
Fatigue Management
Fatigue Policy: Tristar developed policies on drivers’ working, driving and rest hours to ensure they do not feel fatigued while on the road.
Fatigue Monitoring: Fatigue monitoring devices are installed in the vehicle, which allows the detection of early signs of fatigue and alerts the driver and the base monitoring station for any fatigue related issue.
02
Route Hazard Mapping (RHM)
Authorized Route: Tristar carries out route hazard mapping of all authorized routes. The threats and control measures identified are recorded in a stipulated format along with site pictures. Authorized routes are assessed periodically to incorporate any changes on road condition in the route assessment records.
Black Spot: Black Spots are identified and clearly marked in all route hazards mapping.
03
Journey Planning
Journey Plan: Tristar developed the Journey Plan for the purpose of controlling the working hours, driving hours, rest hours and also ensuring authorized routes and rest points are followed.
Pre and Post Trip Briefing: Journey Supervisors conduct pre-trip briefings to inform any deviation or development of any unsafe conditions on the road and the driver also report any deviation during the post trip briefing.
04
Journey Monitoring
IVMS Monitoring: All vehicles are equipped with On Board Computer/In-Vehicle Monitoring System to monitor drivers’ live movement. Also ensuring that drivers are not violating set guidelines for safe driving, which includes drivers’ working hours, adhering to the speed limits, harsh braking, harsh acceleration, etc.
Road Checks: Tristar conducts regular spot checks to ensure drivers comply with company’s HSE requirements which include using only authorized routes, safe delivery, adhering to the speed limits, usage of seat belts, applying defensive driving skills, carrying unauthorized passengers, etc.
HSE Management
01
Risk Management
System Approach: Tristar adapted the risk based approach and follows the Plan – Do – Check – Act (PDCA) cycle for each process.
HSE Management: Tristar’s HSE Management System is designed and built on the basis of the four major pillars, which consist of 4E’s: “Education, Engineering, Enforcement, Evaluation”.
02
Emergency Response
Emergency Response Plan: Tristar developed a comprehensive Emergency Response Plan which covers all key scenarios including but not limited to Medical, Fire, Spill/Leak, Vehicle Rollover, Security Incidents, Vehicle Breakdown and Major Collapse due to adverse condition/natural disasters.
Emergency Drills: To ensure the preparedness to deal with any emergency, Tristar conducts regular emergency drills such as mock and Mega drills every year. Authorities and customers are participating in the Mega drill along with the Tristar Emergency Response Team.
03
Incident Management
Incident Reporting: Tristar has a 24/7 hotline to report any incident. Tristar also developed an online portal called ‘HSE Online’ for instant reporting of HSEQ incidents and NMPIs.
Incident Investigation: All the reported incidents shall be formally investigated by the respective HSEQ and Operations Teams to identify the root causes and come up with appropriate corrective actions.
04
Continual Improvement
APEX HSEQ and Sustainability Committee Meeting: Meeting is conducted periodically to review and analyse HSEQ performances, upcoming HSEQ events, actions to improve, and Key Performance Indicator of HSEQ and Operations.
Quarterly Safety Meeting: There is a safety meeting conducted regularly and is attended by all drivers and ground staff. Government authorities and customers are also invited to participate. Refresher on various safety and driving aspects along with learning from incidents are shared. Appreciation certificates and awards are also given to individuals based on their HSE performance during the last quarter.
